The Pillars of Sir C.R. Reddy Educational Institutions

The Initiator & Founding Chairman

Sri Rao Saheb Damaraju Venkata Rao

In the 1940s, the need for a full-fledged college in Eluru town, the headquarters of West Godavari district, was recognized and fulfilled in 1944 when the then Chairman of the Eluru Municipal Council, the late Sri Rao Saheb Damaraju Venkata Rao, took the initiative.

Under his leadership, the Municipal Council mobilized the sympathy and support of donors from Eluru and surrounding areas to raise a fund of 2 lakh 17 thousand for the opening of the college.

Educational institutions play a vital role in transforming the skills of the youth into constructive and useful ones. Sir C. R. Reddy Municipal College was inaugurated on 4th July 1945 under the chairmanship of Rao Saheb Sri Damaraju Venkata Rao.

The college was formally inaugurated on July 4, 1945, by Sir Cattamanchi Ramalinga Reddy, the distinguished educationist and Vice-Chancellor of Andhra University at that time. The institution was named "Sir C.R. Reddy Municipal College" in honor of the Vice-Chancellor. Sri D.S. Subrahmanyam, a renowned physicist, served as the college's founder principal and established strong academic foundations from its inception. The college's early trajectory reflected its founder's vision of inclusive education. Initially operating as a second-grade institution, it steadily progressed to first-grade status in 1951 with affiliation for B.Sc Physics and B.Com courses. Over subsequent decades, the college expanded its academic offerings to include numerous undergraduate and postgraduate programs, establishing itself as a premier institution that served rural and socioeconomically backward student populations.

Celebrating the Enduring Legacy of Late Alluri Bapineedu
Divya Sri Alluri Bapineedu Garu
The versatile intellectual
Alluri Bapineedu garu
  • Born on 17-03-1911
  • Became the President of the District Congress Committee, West Godavari in 1953.
  • Became the first Chairman of the Zilla Parishad, West Godavari in 1959.
  • Elected to the AP Legislative Assembly in 1955 and in 1962 from Kovvur Assembly Segment.
  • Appointed as Chairman of the District Co-operative Marketing Federation, West Godavari during 1968-70.
  • Became the Chairman, Zilla Parishad, West Godavari for the second time in 1970.
  • Elected to the AP Legislative Council in 1976.
  • He was the Chairman of AP Panchyati Raj Chamber during 1968-71.
  • Director, APSRTC during 1965-68.
  • Served as the President of the Governing Body, Sir C R Reddy College Society, Eluru for 3 Decades (1962-1992).
  • He left for heavenly abode on 27th June, 1992.

A Journey of Service and Inspiration

Alluri Bapineedu garu played a significant role as one of the prominent educationists in Andhra Pradesh shaping the landscape of educational institutions in West Godavari and neighbouring districts. Building on the groundwork laid by Bapineedu garu, he steered the organization from relying on donations for basic needs to achieving financial independence and expanding its educational footprint.

As President, he mobilized donor support and swiftly established numerous institutions from kindergarten to postgraduate levels. These catered to students from underprivileged backgrounds in West Godavari and nearby districts. His leadership introduced professional courses like engineering and MBA, supported by well-developed infrastructure—spacious classrooms, advanced laboratories, well-stocked libraries, computer labs, sports facilities, and a gymnasium—all designed to foster a rich learning environment.

The growth of these institutions was driven by the dedication of committed philanthropists, united under his visionary leadership for nearly three decades. Bapineedu garu's lifelong commitment to uplifting weaker sections through education led to the creation of prominent institutions, from Sir C.R. Reddy Public School to Sir C.R. Reddy Engineering College—today celebrated as the Navaratnas. His legacy will continue to inspire future generations as long as education remains a cornerstone of progress.

Philosophy & Impact

Alluri Bapineedu garu's presidency was the beginning of the sustainable development of Sir C.R. Reddy educational institutions and foundation to became an University.

Bapineedu garu had a deep admiration for education and firmly believed in the philosophy that increasing literacy levels would lead to the Nation's economic development at a higher level.

During his tenure as Chairman of the West Godavari District Parishad, he took responsibility for establishing high schools in various parts of the district, thereby bringing the education system closer to the people. At a time when higher education was largely confined to major cities, Bapineedu garu played a key role in making it accessible to the people of the district. He was a founding member of the governing body that initiated the establishment of Sir C.R. Reddy College in Eluru in 1945. In 1962, he took charge as the Chairman of the governing body of Sir C.R. Reddy College and continued in that role until his last breath in 1992.

The Founding Vice-Chancellor

Sir Cattamanchi Ramalinga Reddy

Sir Cattamanchi Ramalinga Reddy (10 December 1880 – 24 February 1951), popularly known as Sir C.R. Reddy, was a towering intellectual figure in modern Indian history—an educationist, political theorist, literary critic, poet, economist, and social reformer who profoundly shaped educational and cultural discourse in India during the 20th century. His multifaceted career spanned academia, literary scholarship, political activism, and institutional leadership, making him one of the most influential thought leaders of pre- and post-independence India.

Vision Behind the Institution

The Sir Cattamanchi Ramalinga Reddy Educational Institutions, commonly referred to as Sir C.R. Reddy Educational Society, represents one of the most significant educational ecosystems in Andhra Pradesh. Founded in 1945, this network of premier educational institutions is headquartered in Eluru, the district headquarters of West Godavari District. Leadership Through the Decades

Late R.V. Rama Rao had served as the President of Sir C R Reddy college after India attained independence. Later, late Venkata Krishna Ranga Rao, Dommeraju Zamindar while serving as the President, supported the faculty and members in every way in the development of the educational institution.

The next president late Veeramachineni Venkata Narayana, also held the responsibilities as the president of the Andhra Pradesh Congress Committee.

Late Alluri Sarvaraya Chowdhury garu took charge as the President of the Governing Body from 1992 and served for about 15 years, and contributed to the introduction of new courses and development of infrastructure.

Eminent leaders such as Late S.B.P.B.K. Satyanarayana Rao was a former Minister in the Central Government, late Mullapudi Harishchandra Prasad was an industrialist and NABARD Director, late Bolla Bulli Ramaiah, former Union Minister, was a distinguished figure who served as a member of the Governing Body.
