BRIEF HISTORY

From a single academic initiative in 1945 to a comprehensive educational ecosystem, Sir C.R. Reddy Educational Institutions has grown into a "Mini-University" serving thousands of students across multiple disciplines and educational levels.

Foundation & Growth

In the 1940s, there was a long-felt need for quality higher education in the headquarters of West Godavari District. Late Sri Rao Saheb Damaraju Venkata Rao, the then Chairman of the Municipal Council, Eluru, took the initiative in 1944. The Municipal Council, under his leadership, enlisted the support of philanthropic citizens and collected Rs. 2,17,000 to establish the college.

The college was inaugurated on 4th July 1945 by Sir Cattamanchi Ramalinga Reddy, a distinguished educationist and then Vice-Chancellor of Andhra University. The institution was named after him as "Sir C.R. Reddy Municipal College" and began functioning as a second-grade college under Founder Principal Sri D.S. Subrahmanyam.

1945 – 1970
The college was raised to First Grade status in 1951 with affiliation for B.Sc. and B.Com. courses. The Pre-University course began in 1957, followed by the three-year degree programme in 1958. The Intermediate course was introduced in 1969-70, and postgraduate courses in Commerce were launched in 1971. The word "Municipal" was dropped in 1947, making it a private management institution.
1971 – 1987
Postgraduate courses in Politics, Economics, and Industrial Relations were started. The prestigious MBA programme began in 1985. New specializations in B.Sc. Computers, B.Sc. Industrial Fisheries, B.A. Social Work, and Vocational courses in Radio & TV and Computers were introduced. B.Ed. and the College of Education were established in 1984, along with a Public School for quality school-level education.
1987 – 1990s
On 11 May 1987, the UGC accorded Autonomous status to Sir C.R. Reddy College. Sir C.R. Reddy Women's College and Junior College for Women were established in 1987 to cater to women's educational needs. Sir C.R. Reddy Polytechnic was started in 1980 to provide technical and professional education. Sir C.R. Reddy College of Engineering was established in 1989, marking entry into premier engineering education.
2000s – Present
Sir C.R. Reddy College of Pharmaceutical Sciences was established in November 2007 to meet the demand for pharmacy education. The institution was accredited with NAAC 'A' Grade in 2005 and re-accredited in 2011 and 2017. In 2009-10, it was recognized as a "College with Potential for Excellence (CPE)" by UGC. The institution group now operates 11 institutions including schools, colleges, and professional education centers, serving over 15,000 students annually.
Legacy & Recognition
Today, Sir C.R. Reddy Educational Institutions stands as a premier "Mini-University" in Andhra Pradesh, recognized for academic excellence, modern infrastructure, advanced laboratories, digital libraries, and a strong alumni network with professionals serving India and abroad. The institution remains committed to quality education with social responsibility and value-based development.
